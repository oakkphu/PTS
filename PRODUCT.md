# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary users are working adults and staff learning Personal Assistant skills through Thanvasu / PTS Academy — people who need to enroll, study Online / Onsite / Hybrid lessons, keep schedules, earn certificates, and stay in a community while juggling real jobs.

Secondary users are PTS admins who manage courses, lessons, class schedules, users, payments, OTP email, and Google Calendar–related operations from the Admin panel.

Guests may browse courses and read the community without signing in; authenticated students complete the full learning loop.

## Product Purpose

PTS Learning is the Thanvasu e-learning product for Personal Assistant training: discover courses, enroll and pay, learn lessons, track schedules (including Google Calendar sync), collect certificates, and participate in community — in one Thai-first web app.

Success looks like a learner finishing a clear path from course discovery → enrollment → learning → certificate without confusion, and an admin reliably operating schedules and content without fighting the UI.

## Positioning

Professional PA training that fits real work life — Online, Onsite, or Hybrid — with schedules, certificates, and community in one trusted place.

## Brand Personality

Warm, competent, and calm. Voice is clear Thai, respectful, and task-oriented — never chatty marketing fluff inside the app shell. Emotionally: confidence and care, not hype.

## Anti-references

Generic purple SaaS dashboards, Inter-everywhere starter kits, cream/terracotta “AI editorial” landing clichés, nested card stacks, yellow “read-only guest” banners as primary UX, and dark-mode glow tool aesthetics. Also avoid treating Admin as a top-nav badge for every user.

## Design Principles

1. **Task over spectacle** — every screen helps enroll, learn, schedule, pay, or certify; decoration never outranks the job.
2. **One PTS voice** — rose primary + soft surfaces stay consistent from Home to Settings to Admin; don’t invent a second brand per page.
3. **Thai-first clarity** — labels and empty states teach the next step in plain language.
4. **Earn familiarity** — use the shared shell (navbar, buttons, panels, forms); don’t invent one-off controls.
5. **Respect guest and student modes** — browsing stays quiet; privileged actions appear after login without shaming banners.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on body text and controls. Support keyboard focus on forms and primary actions. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any motion. Touch targets on mobile should stay usable for working adults on phones.
